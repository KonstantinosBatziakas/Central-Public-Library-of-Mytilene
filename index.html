About the Library

Over a Century of
Knowledge

The Public Central Library of Mytilene is the largest library on Lesbos. Its origins trace back to 1913, following the liberation of the island from Ottoman rule, when the local press first called for the creation of a public library open to all. It was formally established as a Municipal Library by decision of the Mytilene City Council, later becoming a public institution in 1952.

Today the Library is housed in the former Doukarou Clinic building on Smyrnis Street, a neoclassical structure at the heart of Mytilene. It holds over 55,000 volumes, a rare-books collection, the Vernardakis literary archive, the Lefkias historical archive, and the Syropoulos bequest.

Our Collections

Explore the
Collections

01 📚
General Collection
Γενική Συλλογή

Over 55,000 volumes spanning literature, science, history, philosophy and the arts. Open to all registered members free of charge.

02 🏺
Rare Books
Σπάνια Βιβλία

A curated selection of rare and antiquarian volumes, including early printed books and illuminated manuscripts from the Aegean region.

03 ✒️
Vernardakis Archive
Αρχείο Βερναρδάκη

The personal archive of Demetrios Vernardakis (1833–1907), historian and playwright from Lesbos, comprising manuscripts, correspondence and personal documents.

04 🗺️
Lefkias Historical Archive
Ιστορικό Αρχείο Λεφκία

A major archive of historical documents, maps, photographs and press clippings relating to the history of Lesbos from the 19th century to the present.

05 🎓
Academic Journals
Ακαδημαϊκά Περιοδικά

An extensive collection of academic and scholarly journals across all disciplines, including digital access via the openABEKT national network.

06 💻
Digital Catalogue
Ψηφιακός Κατάλογος

Full online catalogue access via Ψηφιακός Ηρόδοτος on the openABEKT platform. Search, reserve and explore the entire collection from any device.
